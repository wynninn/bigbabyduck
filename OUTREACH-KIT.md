# Creator Outreach Kit — Big Baby Duck

## The email (≤90 words, personalize line 1)

> **Subject:** A duck game your viewers can play in 5 seconds (free, browser)
>
> Hi [name] — loved your [specific video, one clause].
>
> I made **Big Baby Duck — Watermelon Worlds**: a free browser game where a giant baby duck eats 1000 watermelons across 15 worlds while hatching a parade of ducklings. There's a T-Rex. There's a walking watermelon named Melon Bob. It has split-screen 2-player on one keyboard.
>
> Play instantly (no download/key): **https://bigbabyduck.com**
>
> 15-second clip attached. If it makes you smile, it's yours to stream — no strings.
>
> — Benjamin

**Attach:** one GIF/webm (T-Rex chase with duckling parade is the strongest). Nothing else. No wishlist asks, no Discord plugs.

## Targeting rules (from research)
- **5K–250K subscribers** — big enough to matter, small enough to read email.
- Niches in order: family/kids gaming, cozy/casual, "weird indie games" variety channels, speedrunners (the timer!), non-English creators (documented higher conversion — the game needs almost no English).
- Volume: 100–300 emails over 2–4 weeks. Expect single-digit-to-low-double-digit videos per 100. ~30% of "yes" replies ghost — normal, don't chase more than once.
- Find them: search YouTube for "browser games", "weird games", "games with my kid", "cozy games" filtered to recent uploads; check who covered Chained Together / A Difficult Game About Climbing / Crossy Road-likes at small scale.

## The speedrun hook (second-wave angle)
After launch, post your own best time and challenge: "Current world record for 1000 melons is X:XX — first streamer to beat it gets [their clip pinned on the site]." Leaderboard-by-honor-system costs nothing and gives creators a *reason* to make the video competitive.
